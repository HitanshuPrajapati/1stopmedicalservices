We handle the full I-693 process in-house — from your initial intake and labs to vaccinations, physical exam, and officially sealed paperwork. Here's everything you need to know.

Step 1

How to Get Started

Give us a call or walk into either of our clinic locations and let us know you'd like to schedule an Immigration Physical Appointment.

Please have the following ready:

Identification Card
Insurance card (if applicable)
All available vaccination records
1

We will use your information to create a medical chart for you.

2

A required intake form called "Brief I-693" will be sent to you by email and phone to complete at your earliest convenience. This information will be used to fill out your official I-693 form — please verify everything is correct.

The Brief I-693 form is also available on our Resources page.

3

Payment will be collected based on your visit type:

Refugee or K Visa
$275
With Insurance
$375
No Insurance
$450
4

Once payment is made and your intake form is complete, call us to schedule your pick-up appointment — at least one week out.

Call Us to Schedule Get the Brief I-693 Form

Step 2

What Happens After Scheduling

1

We generate an I-693 form filled out with the information from your Brief I-693 form.

2

We verify your vaccination records and send you an initial email listing all pending vaccinations and lab orders based on your age. See our Vaccine & Lab Requirements Tool below.

3

Complete your lab tests as soon as possible — and at least 2 business days before your pick-up appointment to avoid rescheduling. See "How to Complete Your Labs" below.

4

Complete any pending vaccinations we inform you of. See "How to Complete Your Vaccinations" below.

5

Once labs and vaccinations are complete with normal results, we will see you on the day of your pick-up appointment for your physical examination (not required for refugee patients) and the signing and sealing of your official paperwork.

6

At the conclusion of your appointment, you will receive:

An official sealed I-693 envelope — mailed to USCIS (or your lawyer if you have one)
A double-sided copy for your personal records

Optional: Immunity Titer Tests

If you believe you were previously vaccinated for Hepatitis B, Varicella, and/or MMR but no longer have records, you can request an immunity titer test to confirm protection. These are added to your standard lab order at $75 each. A positive titer means the vaccine requirement is already met — no re-vaccination needed.

Important Notes:

If you complete labs elsewhere, you are responsible for providing us results as soon as possible.

If all results are normal, no further action is needed for labs.

If the tuberculosis test is positive, a Chest X-Ray will be required. See Step 4 below.

If syphilis or gonorrhea tests are positive, full treatment must be completed before we can issue your official I-693 form.

Step 4 — If Needed

How to Get Your Chest X-Ray

This step only applies if your tuberculosis (QuantiFERON) test comes back positive. A Chest X-Ray is required to rule out an active infection before we can proceed.

1

Our team will monitor your lab results and, if TB is positive, submit a Chest X-Ray order to RAYUS Radiology on your behalf. You'll be notified by phone and email with a copy of the order attached.

2

Use the button below to find the closest RAYUS location and complete the Chest X-Ray there. Results will be sent to us automatically.

3

If you use a different service, you are responsible for providing us the X-Ray results.

4

You'll be notified of the results. At your pick-up appointment, an additional fee will be collected for this process:

Chest X-Ray Fee: $100

Step 5

How to Complete Your Vaccinations

Our team will regularly monitor your vaccination records and keep you updated on what's still required. If you have historical records for any required vaccines, we can use those. If not, you'll need to complete the missing immunizations before your pick-up.

Important: If your vaccination records are in another language, they must be translated into English by an official translation service before we can use them.

You may complete vaccinations at a pharmacy or external clinic, or at our clinic on the day of your appointment. If done elsewhere, send us the records as soon as possible.
